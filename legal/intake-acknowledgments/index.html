Overview

Before your intake is reviewed, you must read and understand the topics below. You agree to this document when you check the box on the final intake step. You sign and submit your intake on the following screen with your typed legal name.

No guarantee of prescription

Completing this intake does not guarantee that a prescription will be issued. Treatment decisions are made solely by a licensed medical provider based on your health information, applicable law, and clinical judgment.

Licensed provider review

A licensed physician, nurse practitioner, or other qualified clinician will review your intake before any treatment decision. You may be asked for additional information, labs, or an in-person visit if your provider determines that telehealth alone is not appropriate.

Medication risks and side effects

GLP-1 and other weight-management medications can cause side effects and serious complications. Common side effects may include nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, abdominal pain, and decreased appetite.

Less common but serious risks may include:

  • Gallbladder disease or pancreatitis
  • Kidney problems, especially with dehydration
  • Allergic reactions
  • Changes in mood or suicidal thoughts (seek care promptly if you notice new or worsening symptoms)
  • Drug interactions with other medications you take

Your provider will discuss benefits, risks, and alternatives before prescribing. Follow dosing instructions and report new or worsening symptoms promptly.

Emergency and urgent care

Beema Health does not provide emergency care. Call 911 or go to the nearest emergency department if you experience:

  • Severe or persistent abdominal pain
  • Signs of allergic reaction (swelling, rash, trouble breathing)
  • Chest pain, fainting, or severe dizziness
  • Trouble breathing
  • Any symptom you believe is a medical emergency

Compounded medications

If a compounded GLP-1 medication is offered, it is prepared by a licensed compounding pharmacy and is not FDA-approved in the same manner as brand-name products. Compounded medications may only be used when legally available in your state and clinically appropriate for you. Your provider will explain how compounded options differ from FDA-approved alternatives.

Accuracy of your information

You confirm that the health information you provide in this intake is accurate and complete to the best of your knowledge. Withholding or misstating information could lead to inappropriate treatment and harm your health.

Telehealth care

You consent to receive evaluation and follow-up care through telehealth when clinically appropriate. This may include secure messaging, questionnaires, and video or phone visits. Telehealth has limitations compared with in-person care.

For full details, see our Telehealth Consent.

Electronic communication

You consent to receive care-related communications electronically through Beema Health, including email, SMS where you opt in, and in-app notifications about your intake, prescriptions, refills, and account.

Storage and use of intake information

You consent to Beema Health securely storing your intake answers, uploaded documents, and related health information so that licensed providers can review your case, coordinate pharmacy fulfillment, and support your ongoing care.

Use and disclosure of your information are described in our Privacy Policy.
